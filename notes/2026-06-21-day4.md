## Experiment 008 Preliminary Error Analysis

Purpose:

Understand the types of errors made by the Experiment 006 model.

Data source:

results/exp007_predictions.csv

Observed error categories:

1. Phonetic substitutions

Examples:

- uchaguzi → ujaguzi
- kihafifu → kiafifu
- mgongano → mgangano
- desemba → disemba

Frequency:

Very common.

2. Word splitting and merging

Examples:

- wanasema → wana sema
- dereva wa gari → dereva wagari

Frequency:

Very common.

3. Named entity instability

Examples:

- Iran → Irani
- Fairmont → Fermont
- Las Kanitas → Laskhanitas

Frequency:

Moderate.

4. Number and quantity errors

Examples:

- 19,500 → 9,500

Frequency:

Less common but potentially high impact.

5. Severe acoustic confusion

Examples:

- mgonjwa → ingunjwa
- ukristo → uprishno
- yesu → yeku

Frequency:

Occasional.

Observations:

Most prediction errors preserve the overall meaning of the sentence. Many mistakes are minor spelling or phonetic variations rather than catastrophic failures.

Conclusion:

The model is generally intelligible despite a WER around 0.28–0.34. Additional data appears to be the most promising direction for further improvement.
