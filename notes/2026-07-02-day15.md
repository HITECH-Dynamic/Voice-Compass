# Exp048 — Results

## Objective
Train the first true multilingual AfriVoices baseline using five cache-friendly languages.

## Dataset
Languages:
- Kikuyu (kik)
- Kalenjin (kln)
- Luo (luo)
- Maasai (mas)
- Somali (som)

Training rows: 396
Evaluation rows: 98

## Runtime Issue Discovered
Initial run failed due to six corrupted audio files.

Solution:
Created a reusable decode-filter utility that removed invalid audio before training.

Bad files removed:
- Train: 4
- Eval: 2

## Final Metrics

500

Epochs:
2.53

Training Loss:
3.5893

Evaluation Loss:
3.2587

WER:
1.0028

Training Runtime:
3548.8 seconds (~59.1 minutes)

## Comparison

Exp047
WER: 1.0492

Exp048
WER: 1.0028

Relative WER improvement:
≈4.4%

## Findings

The multilingual model successfully trained across five languages without degrading overall recognition performance.

The cache-aware dataset loader remained stable.

The decode-filter utility successfully prevented corrupted audio from interrupting training.

The largest runtime cost was evaluation frequency rather than training itself.

## Conclusion

Exp048 establishes the first stable multilingual competition baseline.

Future experiments should reduce evaluation frequency while keeping multilingual training unchanged.

## Final Metrics

2.53

Training Loss:
2.993

Evaluation Loss:
2.7535

WER:
0.9786

Training Runtime:
675 seconds (~11.3 minutes)

## Comparison
Exp051:
- LR: 2e-5
- WER: 0.9786
- Eval Loss: 2.9022

Exp052:
- LR: 3e-5
- WER: 0.9786
- Eval Loss: 2.7535

## Findings
Increasing LR to 3e-5 improved eval loss but did not improve WER.

Gradients appeared somewhat more volatile, including higher grad_norm values and a nan grad_norm early in training.

## Conclusion
Exp052 is a mixed/tie result.

Do not adopt 3e-5 as the active baseline yet.

Keep Exp051 as the safer active baseline:
- learning_rate: 2e-5
- seed: 42
- eval_steps/save_steps: 50
