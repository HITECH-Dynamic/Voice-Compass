# Day 3 Journal

Today I successfully completed Experiment 001. Using Whisper Small on the FLEURS Swahili dataset and a Tesla T4 GPU on Kaggle, I trained for 50 steps and verified that the entire pipeline works end-to-end.

The model loss decreased steadily from approximately 4.98 to 1.21, demonstrating that the training loop is functioning correctly and that the model is learning.

This experiment validated:

* Kaggle GPU setup
* Hugging Face integration
* Weights & Biases experiment tracking
* Seq2SeqTrainer configuration
* Audio preprocessing and feature extraction
* Checkpoint saving and model serialization

Several bugs were encountered and resolved, including:

* Incorrect FLEURS configuration name (`sw_KE` vs `sw_ke`)
* Removal of deprecated `trust_remote_code=True`
* W&B authentication inside Kaggle

This was intentionally a smoke test rather than a performance experiment. No LoRA, augmentation, architecture comparisons, or language balancing were introduced. The focus was solely on proving that the training infrastructure works.

Experiment 001 establishes the foundation upon which all future experiments will be built.


## Experiment 002 Results

Successfully completed a 500-step training run of Whisper Small on the FLEURS Swahili dataset using a Tesla T4 GPU on Kaggle.

Configuration:

* Train examples: 100
* Validation examples: 50
* Steps: 500
* Runtime: ~25.9 minutes

Results:

* Final train loss: 0.269
* Last logged loss: 0.000879
* Epochs completed: 38.46

Observations:

Loss decreased rapidly and approached zero, indicating that the model effectively memorized the small dataset. Increasing training time alone is insufficient when only 100 examples are available.

Conclusion:

The next experiment should keep the architecture and hyperparameters fixed while increasing dataset size from 100 to 1000 examples. This experiment validated that the bottleneck is data rather than training duration.

## Experiment 003 Results

Configuration:

- Model: Whisper Small
- Dataset: FLEURS Swahili
- Train examples: 1000
- Validation examples: 100
- Steps: 500
- Hardware: Tesla T4

Results:

- Runtime: ~26.8 minutes
- Epochs completed: 4
- Final train loss: 0.839
- Last logged loss: 0.231

Observations:

Training remained smooth and stable. Unlike Experiment 002, the loss did not collapse toward zero. Increasing the amount of data from 100 to 1000 examples reduced memorization and produced a more meaningful baseline.

Conclusion:

Data size had a much larger effect than simply increasing training duration. The next step should be to introduce WER evaluation before adding complexity such as LoRA or multilingual training.
